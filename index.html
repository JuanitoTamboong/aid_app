CYBERCRIME NOTICE
Republic Act No. 10175

IMPORTANT CYBERCRIME WARNING: Under Republic Act No. 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012), false emergency reporting through digital applications is a serious cybercrime offense.

Section 4(c)(4): Computer-related forgery and computer-related fraud apply to false digital reports.

Section 6: All crimes defined under the Revised Penal Code, when committed through information and communications technologies, are punishable under this Act.

PENALTIES FOR FALSE DIGITAL REPORTING:

  • Imprisonment: As prescribed by law for cybercrime offenses
  • Fines: As prescribed by law for cybercrime offenses
  • Cybercrime Record: Permanent cybercrime charges
  • Civil Liability: Payment for all damages caused
  • Additional Penalties: May include community service and restitution

Emergency reporting through this system:

  • Is subject to verification by authorities
  • Requires accurate and truthful information
  • Should only be used for genuine emergencies
  • Follows data privacy and security regulations

⚠️ SERIOUS WARNING: False digital reports constitute cybercrime, waste emergency resources, delay response to real emergencies, and endanger lives. This system should ONLY be used for legitimate emergency situations requiring immediate response.
